Longreach

A long and uneventful drive from Emerald to Longreach. The most notable feature of the trip was the number of triple tailer, double decker cattle trucks there are on the road heading south. All loaded. We figure each truck is carring about 200 head.

Our caravan park for the night was in the bush on the outskirts of town, on the banks of the Thomson River. The bird life was teeming. This Longreach post could be sub-titled "The Birds of Longreach"

On the opposite side of the Thomson River from the caravan park is Apex Park which is very popular free camping area. We visited this a few years ago and noticed the feral roosters around the camp. Yep, they are still there and doing well by the look of it.

Late in the afternoon the sky is full of soaring black kites

Occassionaly one swoops low.

Late afternoon stroll through the bush yielded these sightings.

In the morning we took our consitutional walk along the bush track again. And discovered more first time species.
