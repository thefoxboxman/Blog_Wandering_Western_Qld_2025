Camooweal Billabong

Leaving Mt Isa this morning heading to Camooweal. We followed this guy for about a kilometre along the road

About half between Mt Isa and Camooweal is the start of the Barkley Tablelands. Not really a tableland in the true sense, but a huge region of treeless, black soil plains covered in Mitchell grass. The Barkely stretches across the Qld-NT border for hundreds of kilometres.

Just out of Camooweal is a billabong on the Geogringa River which is famous for it's bird life.

Beside the billabong that streches several kilometres, is a free camping area. We stayed here for the afternoon and overnight. Our first night of free camping on the trip
